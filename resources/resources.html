Calculators

Recommended Physical Calculators

  • Scientific: Texas Instruments TI-30X. This calculator is best if you plan on going into a technical field as its display resembles software code.
  • Scientific: Casio FX-991ESPLUS. This calculator's display is far more user-friendly, but it does not at all resemble software code.
  • Graphing: Casio FX-9750GII. Though not particularly great, it's far better than the abominably overpriced no-innovation-for-decades-because-they-have-a-monopoly TI-83 or TI-84. Your best bet? Ask your teacher if you can use a free graphing calculator online instead. (See immediately below.)

Recommended Online Calculators

  • Desmos Graphing Calculator
  • Desmos Scientific Calculator
  • Symbolab - Does your grunt work.
  • Wolfram Alpha - Also can do your grunt work.
How & Why to Memorize Math Facts?

Math Fact Automaticity - Why kids MUST master their math facts and how to help them do it in a fun way.

Rocket Math - A carefully refined math facts fluency program that my kids enjoy so much they ask for it. Advice: Follow the instructions carefully! Years of experience, testing, and iteration have gone into those instructions.

Spaceship Math Worksheets - A free alternative to Rocket Math. It has far fewer features, but still offers incremental practice of a 1 or 2 equations at a time. It follows many of the recommendations of the link above.

Reflex Math - Paid, online games for math fact fluency.

How To Learn Anything

  • Daniel Willingham is an educational psychologist. His website may be plain, but his work is profound and useful, vastly more rigorous than what you typically get in education writing. I especially recommend his book Why Don't Students Like School?
  • How We Learn vs How We Think We Learn is a video by Robert Bjork, another cognitive psychologist. He explains that intuition is frequently our enemy when it comes to learning.
  • Make it Stick is a book about implementing sound study methods. They may be counterintuitive, but they are powerful.

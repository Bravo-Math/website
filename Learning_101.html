Physical Calculators

  • Scientific: Casio FX-991ESPLUS2. This calculator's 2-line display, fractions, and backspace key make it very user-friendly. Recommended for most students.
  • Scientific: Texas Instruments TI-30X. This calculator's plain text display is good for students entering technical fields that require software coding and command lines.
  • Graphing: Casio FX-9750GII. Though not particularly great, it's far better than the abominably overpriced no-innovation-for-decades-because-they-have-a-monopoly TI-83 or TI-84. Your best bet? Ask your teacher if you can use a free graphing calculator online instead. (See Desmos above.)

Math Facts

  • Math Fact Automaticity - Why kids MUST master their math facts and how to help them do it in a fun way.
  • Rocket Math - A carefully refined math facts fluency program that my kids enjoy so much they ask for it. Advice: Follow the instructions carefully! Years of experience, testing, and iteration have gone into those instructions.
  • Spaceship Math Worksheets - A free alternative to Rocket Math. It has far fewer features, but still offers incremental practice of a 1 or 2 equations at a time. It follows many of the recommendations of the link above.
  • Reflex Math - Paid, online games for math fact fluency.
